The history of Computer and Internet

Early Computers

Early computers were massive machines built in the mid-20th century , designed primarily for complex calculations and data processing. Unlike today's compact devices, they used vacuum tubes, punched cards, and magnetic drums. Though slow and expensive, they laid the foundation for modern technology, inspiring advancements in speed, memory, and accessibility worldwide.


Generations of Computers

The generations of computers mark technological progress from vacuum tubes to today's advanced microprocessors. First-generation machines used bulky vacuum tubes, while the second introduced transistors. The third brought integrated circuits, and the fourth used microprocessors. The fifth generation emphasizes AI, high-speed processing,and advanced software, shaping modern digital innovation globally.

Modern Era

The modern era is defined by rapid technological growth, globalization, and innovation shaping everyday life. Advances in communication, medicine, transportation and AI have transformed how people work and interact. It emphasizes knowledge, digital connectivity, and sustainable progress, marking a shift from traditional practices to a more interconnected, progressive world.
